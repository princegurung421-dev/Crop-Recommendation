Technical Documentation

Deep dive into the Mathematics, Model Performance, and Data Analysis behind Smart Crop Recommendation.

Model Architecture

Smart Crop Recommendation utilizes a Random Forest Classifier, an ensemble learning method that constructs a multitude of decision trees during training. It outputs the class that is the mode of the classes (classification) of the individual trees.

Why Random Forest?

  • High Accuracy: Robust to outliers and noise.
  • Non-Linearity: Capable of handling complex, non-linear relationships between soil nutrients and environmental factors.
  • Feature Importance: Provides insights into which factors (e.g., Rainfall, Nitrogen) impact crop suitability the most.

Mathematical Foundation

The core of the Random Forest algorithm relies on Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating) and Feature Randomness.

1. Gini Impurity (Split Criterion)

Used to decide the best split at each node of the decision tree. We minimize the impurity:

$$ Gini(p) = 1 - \sum_{i=1}^{J} p_i^2 $$

Where $p_i$ is the probability of an item being classified for a particular class.
2. Entropy (Information Gain)

Alternative measure for splitting:

$$ H(S) = - \sum_{c \in C} p(c) \log_2 p(c) $$
